Sequential Stern–Gerlach

A beam of spin-½ atoms is first prepared spin-up along Z by the left magnet. A second Stern–Gerlach magnet is rotated by an angle θ relative to the first. Classically you would expect a continuous smear; quantum mechanics predicts the beam splits into exactly two discrete spots, with probabilities cos²(θ/2) (up) and sin²(θ/2) (down).

▲ P(up) = 0.85     ▼ P(down) = 0.15
