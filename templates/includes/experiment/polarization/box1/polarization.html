Three Polarizing Filters — interactive (Malus's law)

Unpolarized light hits three polarizers in a row. With filter A vertical (0°) and filter C horizontal (90°), no light gets through. Insert filter B at 45° between them and light reappears — because a polarizer doesn't just block, it re-projects the polarization. Each stage follows Malus's law I = I₀ cos²(Δθ).

Meter reading: 12.5% of the source  (25.0% of the light that passed filter A)
