Ramsey Interferometer — separated oscillatory fields

A two-level atom starts in the ground state. Two π/2 pulses, separated by a free-evolution time T, drive it. During T the atomic phase advances at the detuning δ (= drive frequency − atomic frequency). The chance of finding the atom excited oscillates as Pe(δ) = cos²(δT/2) — the Ramsey fringes below. Longer T makes the fringes finer, which is exactly why Ramsey's method underpins atomic clocks.
